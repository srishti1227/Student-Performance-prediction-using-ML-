Exam Pattern Information

The SAT exam pattern is a standardized test used by most US universities for their admissions process. The SAT evaluates an applicant based on their skills in mathematics, evidence-based reading, and writing. The SAT exam pattern consists of two main sections:
Evidence-Based Reading and Writing (EBRW) and Math, each scored on a range of 200-800.
The EBRW section tests your reading comprehension, grammar, and vocabulary skills, while the Math section tests your algebra, geometry, trigonometry, and data analysis skills.
The SAT also has an optional Essay section, which tests your ability to analyze a source text and write a persuasive essay.
Here is a breakdown of the SAT exam pattern:
Reading: 52 multiple-choice questions, 65 minutes, score range 200-800
Writing and Language: 44 multiple-choice questions, 35 minutes, score range 200-800
Math (with calculator): 38 questions, 55 minutes, score range 200-800
Math (no calculator): 20 questions, 25 minutes, score range 200-800
Total: 154 questions, 180 minutes, score range 400-1600.
